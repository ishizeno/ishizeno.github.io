Status

ISHIZENO i8 is currently under development. We are now in the process of gathering requirements and validating some of the core technologies that are planned to be used for the system, like the Adapteva E16G301 16-core microprocessor. If we do not hit any major roadblock, we hope to complete a first prototype this year, and a small production batch next year.

ISHIZENO i8 is designed by Ismael Ghalimi, who is neither a musician nor a synthesizer builder. Instead, he applies his expertise with software development and his fresh perspective on sound processing to develop something totally new and radical.

Architecture

The ISHIZENO i8 is built around 7 customizable printed circuit boards: PSU, audio ADC converters, audio DAC converters, analog CV converters (ADC and DAC), mesh network, AVB network, and headphones amplifier. The mesh network backplane itself hosts four Parallella-16 submodules, offering a combined 72 CPU cores and 880 DSP slices.

Parallel Architecture

Each of the four Parallella-16 boards includes a Xilinx Zynq-7020 FPGA and an Adapteva E16G301 16-core microprocessor. The Zynq runs the Linux operating system on an ARM dual-core Cortex-A9 processor, and provides 85K logic cells, or 220 DSP slices. The Adapteva is used as co-processor.

Through the backplane, the 4 Zynq devices are connected over an AVB Gigabit Ethernet network, while the 4 Adapteva devices are connected through a high-performance mesh network with a square topology offering 8 GB/s off-chip bandwidth and 3 ns latency.

Audio Synthesis

The patching of audio and CV signals is handled in the digital domain through the mesh network. All 16 audio inputs and 16 CV inputs are available to all 16 CPU cores of all four submodules for audio synthesis. Also, 16 CV outputs can be produced and converted into analog signals.

Each Parallella-16 board is responsible for producing two stereophonic outputs which are routed to the digital-to-analog audio converters. Additionally, the 8 stereophonic audio outputs are mixed in the digital domain, then converted into a pair of analog audio signals.

Audio Mixing

The faceplate includes 9 motorized faders with 64-LED bargraphs, 8 knobs with RGB illumination and SPST switching, one 40mm jog dial, 9 transport control buttons, 32 buttons for Mute, Solo, Sel, and Rec, and 5 buttons that can be dynamically assigned. All buttons are illuminated.

Mixing is done in the digital domain for the 8 stereophonic audio outputs and a virtually unlimited number of digital audio channels that can be produced by the 64 CPU cores offered by the 4 Adapteva devices, and the 880 DSP slices offered by the 4 Zynq devices.

  • Online services implemented using the Google Cloud Platform

G3 Grid

The g3 is an optional companion to the i8.
324 keys and 16 pads sensitive to pressure and velocity.
8 customizable controls for mode selection and track transport.


Length: 910mm (35.83")
Width: 215mm (8.36")
Height: 3.14cm (1.24")

While the module can be used with any USB controller, it is being developed jointly with a grid controller, the ISHIZENO g3. This grid is made of 324 keys, 16 drum pads, and 8 controls. Keys, pads, and controls are illuminated by RGB leds and are sensitive to pressure and velocity.

The controller is made of a black walnut frame and is controlled by an embedded Raspberry Pi 2. It also provides a longitudinal groove capable of holding up to 8 tablets in a semi-vertical position, and can be extended with left and right docks for hosting one or two tablets horizontally.
